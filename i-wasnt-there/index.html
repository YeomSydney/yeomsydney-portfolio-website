I Wasn't There

Absence speaks louder than words.

It is a set of two booklets enclosed in a box cover, exploring the themes of absence and existence through two different poems. It examines how memory, imagination, and personal histories are shaped in the absence of direct experience and how these gaps inform our understanding of existence.

Details

Year 2024
Duration 4 Weeks
Category
  • Editorial
Industry Poem Commentary
Tools

CHALLENGE

Exploring the concepts of absence and existence, I reinterpreted them by three poems and a series of black-and-white photographs, presenting how these two concepts can be perceived across multiple mediums.

The main challenge was translating abstract concepts – absence and existence — across multiple mediums, including photography, poetry, and editorial writing. This required a clear understanding of the themes before developing a cohesive visual and narrative system.

To address this, I curated photographs that visually reflect these concepts and selected poems from authors with diverse perspectives. I also incorporated some commentaries to connect these elements, emphasizing that absence and existence are not defined by a single viewpoint, but shaped collectively across different contexts. Ultimately, it was needed to distill these complex ideas into a format that can be both visually engaging and conceptually clear for the reader.
